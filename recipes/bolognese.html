Bolognese

Bolognese is a rich, slow-simmered Italian meat sauce with deep, savory flavors. Made with a blend of ground meat, aromatic vegetables, tomatoes, and a touch of wine and milk, this hearty sauce develops a velvety texture over time. Perfectly paired with pasta like tagliatelle or pappardelle, itâ€™s a comforting and classic dish that brings out the best in traditional Italian cooking.

Ingredient List

  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 small onion
  • 1 carrot
  • 1 celery stalk
  • 2 gloves garlic
  • 1 lb ground beef
  • 1/2 cup dry white wine
  • 1 cup whole milk
  • 1 can (28 0z) crushed tomatoes
  • 2 tsbp tomato paste

Directions

  1. Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrot, and celery, cooking until softened. Stir in garlic and cook for another minute.
  2. Add ground beef and cook, breaking it up with a spoon, until browned. Drain excess fat if necessary.
  3. Pour in the wine and simmer until mostly evaporated. Stir in the milk and let it cook down.
  4. Add crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, salt, pepper, oregano, bay leaf, and nutmeg. Stir well.
  5. Reduce heat to low and let simmer uncovered for 1.5 to 3 hours, stirring occasionally and adding broth if it gets too thick.
  6. Cook pasta according to package instructions. Drain and toss with the sauce.
  7. Serve hot, topped with grated Parmesan. Enjoy your rich, hearty Bolognese!
