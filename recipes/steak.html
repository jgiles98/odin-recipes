Pan Seared Steak

A perfectly pan seared steak is a thing of beauty, crispy golden-brown crust on the outside, tender and juicy on the inside, bursting with rich, buttery flavor. The high heat sear locks in the juices, while garlic and fresh herbs infuse every bite with mouthwatering depth. It's the kinf of steak that makes your kitchen smell like a high end steakhouse and has you savoring every forkful like it's a special occasion.

Ingredient List

  • 1 well-marbled steak (ribeye,filet mignon, or New York strip)
  • Salt (coarse or flaky)
  • Freshly ground black pepper
  • 1-2 Tbsp high smoke point oil (avocado oil or vegetable oil)
  • 2 Tbsp unsalted buttery
  • 2-3 garlic cloves, smashed
  • 1-2 sprigs fresh rosemary or thyme

Directions

  1. Prepare the steak -Pat the steak dry with paper towels and season generously with salt and black pepper. Let it sit at room temperature for about 30 minutes to ensure even cooking.
  2. Heat the pan -Place a cast iron skillet over high heat and add the oil. Let it heat until just smoking.
  3. Sear the Steak -Carefully place the steak in the hot pan and let it sear undisturbed for 2-3 minutes untila deep golden-brown crust forms. Flip and sear other side for another 2-3 minutes.
  4. Add Butter & Aromatics - Reduce the heat to medium low and add the butter, smashed garlic, and fresh rosemary or thyme. As the butter melts, tilt the pan slightly and use a spoon to baste the steak repeatedly.
  5. Finish Cooking - Continue cooking, flipping occasionally and basting, until the steak reacges ypur desired doneness (125 F for rare, 135 F for medium rare, etc).
  6. Rest & serve - Transfer the steak to a plate and let it rest for 5-10 minutes to allow juices to redistribute. Slice and enjoy!
